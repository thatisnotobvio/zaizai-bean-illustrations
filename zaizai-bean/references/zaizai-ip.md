# ZaiZai Bean

## Role Definition

ZaiZai Bean is the permanent visual IP used in ZaiZai's English text illustrations.
By default, ZaiZai Bean must appear in every image. She is not a mascot, not a sticker, not a cute decoration, but a system operator who possesses the mindset of both a Product Designer (not a UI designer) and a Product Manager. She connects user needs, business goals, technological limitations, and product decisions. 

## shape

- Brown solid little bean.
- White dotted eyes.
- Slender legs, and occasionally slender arms.
- The body can be a cylinder, a brown bean, a brown box, a funnel, a shadow, a hole, or a brown hole inside a machine.
- The outline is slightly irregular, giving it a hand-drawn feel.
- Her expression was blank, dazed, calm, and serious.
- She can be naturally integrated into machines, processes, diagrams, pipelines, and system structures.

## identity

ZaiZai Bean is :
- Product Designer
- Product Thinker
- System Builder
- Decision Operator

She is responsible for:
- Understand the user.
- Problem identified.
- Organize information.
- Establish the structure.
- Make a judgement.
- Drive product implementation.

## character

- She's very serious, but extremely pragmatic.
- Trust evidence over opinion.
- A bit clumsy but very reliable.
- Always solving problems.
- Dry humor, no attempts to be cute.
- It's like actually being in charge of a certain task in a whiteboard sketch.

## Common Responsibilities

Let ZaiZai Bean take on the core actions:
### User Research
- Dissecting the "user feedback fish".
- Collect user voice recordings.
- Extract insights from chaotic material.
- Use a magnifying glass to observe user behavior.

### Problem Definition
- Piece together the scattered problems into a complete map.
- Label the chaos.
- Identify the real pain points.
- Filter out the core issues from the noise.

### Information Architecture
- Content transfer module.
- Build the structural skeleton for the page.
- Organize the messy wiring.
- Put the functionality into the right container.

### User Journey
- Bridge between fracture paths.
- Repairing any missing steps.
- Guide users through complex processes.
- Connect different nodes by pulling the flow line.

### Priority Judgement
- Operate the Impact lever.
- Operate the Effort lever.
- Operate the Risk lever.
- Adjust the weights on the unbalanced scale.
- Place the Feature into different priority bins.

### Product Strategy
- Planning route map.
- Move the Now / Next / Later flags.
- Find your way on the map.
- Observe the future path.

### Cross-team Collaboration
- Pull on ropes coming from different directions.
- Coordinate conflicting objectives.
- Multiple inputs are converged into one output.
- Connecting users, business, and technology.

### Data Analysis
- Measuring indicator: liquid level.
- Record growth dashboard.
- Machine for adjusting conversion rates.
- Observe the flow of data.

### Product Delivery
- Machines for assembling products.
- Inspect the version of the rocket that is about to be launched.
- Repair the last breakpoint.
- Waiting next to the online button.

## Common Metaphors

Let ZaiZai Bean be the core action subject in the metaphor:
- Treat user feedback like dissecting a fish.
- Transporting content within the information pipeline.
- Operating the decision-making machine.
- Use a funnel to filter requirements.
- Connect the team using a pulley system.
- Construct bridges along user pathways.
- Maintain the product production line.
- Adjust the route map and track.
- Record the water level indicators.
- Priority labels are assigned to features.
- Acting as an operator within a complex system.

## prohibit

- Don't draw ZaiZai Bean as an overly cute mascot.
- Do not draw children's cartoon characters.
- Don't give ZaiZai Bean complicated outfits, emojis, or bright eyes.
- Don't let ZaiZai Bean just stand in the corner and watch.
- Don't let the little brown thing steal the structural expression.
- Don't draw ZaiZai Bean too commercially, too rounded, or too delicately.
- Don't let ZaiZai Bean pretend to work while holding the computer.

## Judgement criteria

If we remove the little brown bean, the core metaphor of the image still holds true, which means the little brown bean is too decorative; we need to rewrite the prompts to make the little brown bean the subject of the action.
