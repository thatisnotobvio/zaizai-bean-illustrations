# Composition Patterns and Originality Rules

## Basic structure types

Choose one structure; don't mix too many.
### Workflow
Suitable for: Input -> Processing -> Output, Content Production, AI Workflow, Product workflows.

Drawing method: Input on the left, processing by the small brown bean or strange machine in the middle, output on the right, and orange arrows indicate the main direction.

### System Local
Suitable for: Information sources, Filtering systems. Databases, Rendering engines, Agent systems, Product subsystems.

Drawing method: left side chaotic, right side stable, orange arrow in the middle. Avoid drawing the entire architecture. The character can be more exaggerated.

### Before and after comparison
Suitable for: chaotic/orderly, Manual/automated, scattered/organized, confused/clarity, Weak product/ Strong product.

Drawing style: left side chaotic, right side stable, orange arrow in the middle. The character can be more exaggerated.
  
### Character Status
Suitable for: user pain points, product team struggles, decision paralysis, tool overload, information anxiety, getting stuck and starting to run.

Drawing method: 2-4 small states, each with a short label.

### Conceptual Metaphor
Suitable for: product systems, decision-making engines, feedback loops, user research pipelines, product factories, internal reasoning systems.

Drawing method: A large monster or machine, a few inputs, and one output. It should have a memorization point.

### Method layering
Suitable for: frameworks, capability stacks, product maturity models, system layers, organisational thinking.

Drawing method: Layers of boxes, not a formal pyramid; Little Black is moving bricks or building on the side.

### Map route
Suitable for: product roadmaps, user journeys, learning paths, idea-to-launch journeys, adoption paths.

Drawing method: A curved path, key milestones, a few nodes, and small black lines or walking paths.

### Mini comic storyboard
Suitable for: Failure to success, real-world process, product evolution, product transformation, storytelling.

Drawing method: 2-4 small scenes, each cell expressing only one action.

## Original Metaphor Generation Method

Each time, the metaphor is reinvented from the current article; old images cannot be copied verbatim.
### Three steps
1. Transform the abstract concept into a physical action: stuck, leaked, becoming heavier, sorting, settling, fermenting, opening, folding, unpacking, and recirculation.
2. Replace the system structure with a low-tech object: a broken machine, a cardboard box, a drawer, a water pipe, a gate, a conveyor, a mailbox, a strange dial, a scale, a well, a ladder, and a strange workstation.
3. Let ZaiZai Bean perform the actions: instead of standing by, it gets stuck in the machine, pulls the wrong wire, guards the door, moves things, repairs, weighs things, holds the ladder, recording decisions, routing users through a system, and stuffs things into some strange device.

### Available object pool
- Cardboard boxes, drawers, old machines, funnels, scales, mailboxes, doors, wells, ladders, water pipes, spools of yarn, gates, turntables, black boxes, hole punches, noodle presses, clotheslines, mechanical switchboard, product roadmap, priority sorter and odd workstations.
- Select only 1-2 items at a time, and do not pile them up.

### Brown Action Pool
- Pull, carry, stuff, scoop, press, weigh, sew, cut, twist, guard, push, insert, lift, repair, route, sort, record, assemble, priortize, organize, measure, patch, guide, deliver, connect, dismantle, mark, recycle.
- Actions should serve the core meaning; don't be strange for the sake of being strange.

## Repeatedly engraving rules

Do not open or copy by default `assets/examples/`. These images are only used for style calibration, such as line density, white space, color restraint, and a minimalist aesthetic.

Unless the user explicitly says "copy this/reproduce this composition/modify this example", do not directly reuse these old compositions:
- Conveyor belts with two breakpoints
- ZaiZai Bean pulling a decision lever inside a machine
- ZaiZai Bean acting as a funnel
- ZaiZai Bean leads the path
- ZaiZai Bean dissecting a feedback fish
- ZaiZai Bean dragging a user journey path
- ZaiZai Bean pulling information sources together
- Multiple ZaiZai Beans holding megaphones, building bridges, or opening doors
- ZaiZai Bean stamping a messaging toolbox
- ZaiZai Bean holding warning signs beside common pitfalls

Even for similar themes, new metaphors should be used. For example, Instead of drawing a roadmap as a path, draw ZaiZai Bean physically moving future milestones between tracks; Instead of showing prioritization as a funnel, draw ZaiZai Bean weighing features on an unstable machine; Instead of showing content repurposing as a fish, draw ZaiZai Bean compressing one paper object into several different outputs; Every illustration should feel newly discovered rather than reused.
