# Style DNA

## In short

Pure white, minimalist, hand-drawn, white space, restraint, bizarre, product sketch feel, English handwriting feel, clear structure but no instruction manual.

It's like a rough explanatory sketch drawn casually on a blank sheet of paper by someone who has long worked in AI, product development, design, and development tools.

## must

- 16:9 horizontal text with accompanying images.
- Pure white background: Avoid beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, and retro paper feel.
- Primarily black hand-drawn line art: fine lines, slight trembling, not mechanical, not vector-like, and not heavy outlines.
- Ample white space: The main subject occupies about 40%-60% of the image, with at least 35% blank space, ideally a whole quiet area.
- A small number of handwritten English annotations: a maximum of 5-8 places, each with 2-8 characters if possible.
- A single diagram should convey only one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- The structure should be expressed naturally; do not write the structure type name on the diagram.

## color

- Black: Main line art, character legs and arms, borders, structure, main text, main objects.
- Brown: character body.
- Red: Important annotations, warnings, friction points, pain points, critical outcomes, key decisions.
- Orange: primary flows, movement, process paths, directional arrows, cause-and-effect transition, system movement from A to B.
- Blue: Secondary explanations, internal reasoning, system states, contextual notes, AI behavior, Assistent logic, Automation indicators.

Blue isn't necessary in every design. Use color sparingly; less is more.

## Never

- No commercial illustrations.
- No PowerPoint infographics.
- Do not use formal flowcharts.
- Do not use course materials.
- No cute cartoon posters.
- No children's illustrations.
- Avoid complex architecture diagrams.
- Avoid overly elaborate flat illustrations.
- No tech-savvy UI.
- Do not use screenshots of real apps.
- Avoid complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, and textures.
- Don't explain every single node in detail.
- Do not use titles such as "Workflow Diagram / System Architecture Diagram / Common Pitfalls / Roadmap" in the top left corner.

## Aesthetic direction

It needs to be bizarre, creative, interesting, simple and refreshing, and imaginative.

Avoid cuteness, childishness, complexity, and rigidity.
